Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam – The Missile Man of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, widely known as APJ Abdul Kalam, was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, in a humble family.

Early Life and Education

Dr. Kalam’s early life was full of struggles and dedication. Despite financial difficulties, he worked hard in school. He studied physics and aerospace engineering and later joined DRDO and ISRO, India’s top research organizations.

Contribution to Science and Technology

He played a key role in India’s nuclear tests in 1998 and was known as the “Missile Man of India” for his work on ballistic missile technology. He led projects like Agni and Prithvi missiles, which made India a strong nation in defense.

President of India

In 2002, Dr. Kalam became the 11th President of India. He was called the “People’s President” because he was loved by all, especially students. He focused on education, technology, and youth development.

Vision for India

Dr. Kalam believed in a developed India. He wrote many inspiring books like “Wings of Fire” and “India 2020”. He once said, "Dream is not that which you see while sleeping, it is something that does not let you sleep."

Legacy and Inspiration

Even after his term as president, he continued to meet students and inspire them. He passed away on 27th July 2015 while delivering a lecture, doing what he loved the most — teaching.

Summary

Born: 15th October 1931

Famous For: Missile Technology and Scientific Leadership

President of India: 2002–2007

Died: 27th July 2015
